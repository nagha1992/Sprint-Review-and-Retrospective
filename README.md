# CS 250 Portfolio: SNHU Travel Project

Essential Questions Reflection

1. Interpreting User Needs and User Stories
I interpret user needs by transitioning from large, monolithic requirements to granular user stories. By defining work from the end-user's perspective—such as "As a traveler, I want to filter flights by price"—the team is able to focus on maximizing value for the customer. Creating user stories helps ensure that testing and feedback occur continuously throughout the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC), allowing the team to validate functionality early and reduce the risk of large-scale failures.

2. Developing Programs and Agile Processes
I approach program development through an iterative process that prioritizes adaptability over rigid predictability. In my future development work, I hope to incorporate Agile processes such as **Sprint Planning** to define clear 2-week deliverables and **Daily Scrums** to synchronize activities and identify obstacles. I also value the **Sprint Retrospective** as a critical space for the team to reflect on process improvements and maintain a high standard of quality.

3. Being a Good Team Member in Software Development
To be a good team member in software development means acting as a "servant leader" who focuses on removing impediments and facilitating key Scrum events to ensure transparency. It requires being part of a self-organizing, cross-functional unit that takes ownership of technical execution. Effective team members maintain open communication and utilize organizational tools like Jira to ensure the team’s progress and challenges are visible to all stakeholders.
